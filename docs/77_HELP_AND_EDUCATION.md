# Help & Education

Version 2.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Harmonix educates users within the product.

Education is not a separate help center.

It is woven into every part of the interface.

The goal: users never feel confused, and never have to leave the app to understand it.

---

# Education Philosophy

## Teach in context, not in advance.

Don't frontload education.

Show the explanation when the user is looking at the thing.

---

## Respect user intelligence.

DeFi users are not beginners.

But even experienced users encounter new terms.

Keep explanations concise and precise — not dumbed down.

---

## The education ladder.

Level 1: Tooltip (1–2 sentences — what it is).

Level 2: Inline callout (3–5 sentences — why it matters).

Level 3: Modal or side panel (full explanation with examples).

Level 4: External link (full article, for deep readers).

Never skip levels. Don't send a user to a full article for a single term.

---

# Glossary Tooltips

Every metric, term, or technical label in the UI should have a tooltip.

Triggered by: `?` icon or underline on the term.

---

## Tooltip Format

```
[Term] ⓘ

What it is: One sentence.
Why it matters: One sentence.
```

Maximum 2 sentences. No exceptions.

---

## Core Glossary

| Term | Tooltip |
|------|---------|
| APY | Annual Percentage Yield — the estimated yearly return if current rates hold. Higher APY means more earnings per year. |
| TVL | Total Value Locked — the total amount deposited in this vault by all users. Higher TVL often indicates user confidence. |
| Delta Neutral | A strategy that hedges directional risk. Returns come from funding rates, not price movement. |
| Funding Rate | A periodic payment between long and short positions in perpetuals markets. Positive rates mean shorts pay longs. |
| Max Drawdown | The largest peak-to-trough decline in vault value. Lower is better — indicates less historical loss. |
| Sharpe Ratio | Risk-adjusted return. How much return per unit of risk. Higher is better. |
| Pending Withdrawal | A withdrawal you've requested that is being processed by the vault operator. Funds are leaving the strategy. |
| Vault Capacity | The maximum amount a vault can hold. Some vaults cap deposits to maintain strategy efficiency. |
| Operator | The entity that manages the vault's trading strategy. Responsible for performance and security. |
| Yield | The earnings generated by a vault, distributed to depositors. |
| Position | Your active investment in a vault — the amount deposited plus accumulated yield. |
| Lock-up Period | Time you must wait before withdrawing. Harmonix vaults currently have no lock-up. |
| Slippage | The difference between expected and actual transaction price. Can reduce received amount. |

---

## Tooltip Trigger Design

Term with tooltip: underline with dotted style.

`?` icon: 14px circle, `color-text-tertiary`.

Hover (desktop): tooltip appears after 300ms delay.

Tap (mobile): tooltip appears immediately, dismissed by tapping elsewhere.

Tooltip position: above by default, flips below if near top edge.

## Getting Started FAQ

**Q: What is Harmonix?**

Harmonix is a yield management platform on Hyperliquid. You deposit assets into vaults, and professional strategies earn yield on your behalf. You retain full ownership — we never custody your funds.

**Q: Do I need to create an account?**

No. Connect your wallet and you're ready to deposit. No email, no KYC, no sign-up.

**Q: Is Harmonix custodial?**

No. Your assets are held in smart contracts, not by Harmonix. You can withdraw at any time.

---

## Deposits FAQ

**Q: What assets can I deposit?**

Currently USDC on Hyperliquid. More assets coming in future vaults.

**Q: Is there a minimum deposit?**

Yes, each vault has its own minimum. Most start at $100 USDC.

**Q: Can I deposit into multiple vaults?**

Yes. Each vault is independent. You can have positions in as many vaults as you like.

**Q: Why do I need to approve USDC first?**

This is a standard ERC-20 step. Before a smart contract can move your tokens, you must approve it once. This approval is specific to the vault contract. It's a one-time step per asset.

---

## Withdrawals FAQ

**Q: How long does withdrawal take?**

2–5 days depending on the vault strategy. The operator needs time to unwind the position safely.

**Q: Do I need to "claim" my funds after withdrawal?**

No. Funds transfer automatically to your wallet when processing completes. No action needed.

**Q: Can I cancel a withdrawal after submitting?**

No. Once submitted, withdrawals enter the processing queue and cannot be cancelled.

**Q: What if my withdrawal takes longer than expected?**

Check the status in your Portfolio. If it's significantly delayed, contact support with your transaction ID.

---

## Vault Performance FAQ

**Q: Is APY guaranteed?**

No. APY is based on historical performance and current market conditions. It changes daily.

**Q: What is Max Drawdown?**

The largest loss the vault experienced from a peak. It shows the worst-case historical decline.

**Q: What is delta neutral?**

A strategy that hedges directional price exposure. Returns come from funding rates and market making, not from price appreciation or decline.

---

## Security FAQ

**Q: Has Harmonix been audited?**

Yes. Smart contracts are audited by [audit firm]. Audit reports are available at [link].

**Q: What happens if there's an exploit?**

Operations are paused immediately. Users are notified. Full incident disclosure is published. See `72_CONTINGENCY_AND_RISK_COMMUNICATION.md`.

**Q: Can Harmonix access my funds?**

Vault contracts are permissioned but not custodial. Harmonix cannot move funds to an arbitrary address.

---

# Education Anti-Patterns

Never show more than one tooltip at a time.

Never show education content for things the user clearly already knows (e.g., don't explain APY to a user who has used 5 vaults).

Never use education as a reason to delay the primary action.

Never show education pop-ups at app start.

Never make users read a primer before depositing.

---

# Final Education Principle

Education is not about covering liability.

Education is about making users feel capable.

A user who understands what they're investing in is more confident.

A confident user deposits more.

A confident user refers others.

The best education is invisible — users just feel like they always knew.
